Application Area

  • Solar cookers can be used in “no burn” areas and on “no burn” days. So it is perfect for camping in areas with fire restrictions.
  • It can be used anywhere a hot meal is needed or desired such as mountain tops, on sailboats, camping and tailgating.
  • Pasteurizing water in a solar cooker before consumption by raising the temperature to 151 degrees Fahrenheit (66 degrees Celsius) is an effective way to purify water.
  • In countries where clean water is scarce, infections that develop as a result of improperly sterilized medical equipment are common. Chemical disinfectants, such as iodine, are seldom available, and fuel for boiling water is expensive. In such places, solar cooker provide a cheap and available alternative to wood and petroleum for heating water.

Advantages Of ETC

  • It is portable and can be taken almost anywhere.
  • Requires less water to cook because the water does not boil away taking valuable nutrients with it.
  • Burning wood, charcoal, fossil fuels, and other types of fuel contributes to the ever-increasing global air pollution. However, in the context of solar cooking, since there is no fire or flames involved, it results in minimal air pollution.
  • All types of food can be cooked within 30 minutes to 1 hour, which is 4 to 6 times faster than box-type solar cookers, depending on the amount of sun radiation available.
  • It is useful for cooking, boiling, shallow frying food, and also for heating milk.
  • Fast feedback period, no maintenance, long life, operational safety, easy to handle and eco-friendly.
